# Current Capstone Project

## Improving Veteran Healthcare Through Mortality Analytics

**Dataset**

2018–2023 VA Veteran All-Cause Mortality Dataset

**Business Problem**

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) continues to face significant challenges in reducing preventable mortality among veterans while ensuring healthcare resources are directed where they can have the greatest impact. Although chronic diseases such as heart disease and cancer account for many veteran deaths, suicide and unintentional injuries contribute disproportionately to premature mortality, particularly among younger veterans.

A key question for the Veterans Health Administration (VHA) is whether veterans who recently utilized VHA healthcare services experience different mortality patterns than veterans who did not receive recent VHA care. Understanding these differences may help identify high-risk populations, evaluate healthcare utilization patterns, and support evidence-based decisions for suicide prevention, chronic disease management, and healthcare planning.

This capstone applies health informatics and healthcare analytics to transform publicly available mortality data into meaningful insights that can support population health management, strategic planning, and continuous quality improvement across the Veterans Health Administration.

## Data Journey

- Selected the 2018–2023 VA Veteran All-Cause Mortality Dataset.
- Evaluated data quality and dataset limitations.
- Cleaned and standardized annual worksheets.
- Prepared the data for longitudinal analysis.
- Determined that descriptive, comparative, and time-series analysis were the most appropriate analytical approaches.

## Solution

The project uses:

- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Descriptive Statistics
- Comparative Analysis
- Time-Series Trend Analysis
- Data Visualization using Python and Microsoft Excel
